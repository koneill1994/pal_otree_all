Purpose: The purpose of this study is to look at some factors that affect learning in different conditions. Some of the factors are interacting with the content, interacting with other learners, and choosing extracurricular activities. This study is designed to simulate the daily life of a student with a combination of home time and school time.

You will have 6 sessions, each with simulated home time followed by simulated class time. Each session will have 4 minutes of home time, followed by class time working on your task.

For home time, you may choose to study, or to play a game, or to do nothing.

  • studying will help you do better in school time

For school time, your task is to solve word puzzles. You must find the right word for each set of words, and type it in the blank.

  • you will be given three words; the answer will be a word that goes with all three of them
  • here's an example: age / mile / sand
  • the answer is stone because it makes a compound word with each of the first three words:
    • stoneage
    • milestone
    • sandstone
  • you will have 30 seconds for each set of words
  • you will also need to rate how confident you are about your answer
  • you get to provide more guesses if you're not sure about your answer

You might be in a group with three other players. After you give your answer, you can hover over the other players' answers and choose one of those if you want. They can also choose yours or someone else's if they want. You will get points for how accurate your group is.

After the 6 sessions of home and school time, you will have a 7th session like a final exam. The final exam is to see how many of the word puzzles you know how to solve and how quickly you can solve them.

After the final exam, you will receive a final score. This score is made up of points you get over the whole study.

  • you can get up to 20 points over the whole time for playing games
  • you can get up to 10 points over the whole time for getting the first answer in school time
  • you can get up to 40 points for the final exam
  • if you are in a group, you can get up to 30 points more depending on how accurate your group is
